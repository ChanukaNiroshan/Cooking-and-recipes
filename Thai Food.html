Ingredients


1 lb (450g) ground pork, 2 cups Thai holy basil leaves, 4-5 cloves garlic, minced, 2-3 Thai bird chilies, finely chopped, 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, 1 tablespoon oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 teaspoon fish sauce, 1 teaspoon sugar, Jasmine rice, for serving, Fried egg , for serving

Cooking instructions


Prepare Ingredients:
Rinse the basil leaves and set them aside. Mince the garlic and chop the Thai bird chilies.
Cook Rice: Start by cooking jasmine rice according to the package instructions. This will ensure that your rice is ready when the stir-fry is done.
Stir-Fry:
Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic and chopped Thai bird chilies to the hot oil. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds or until fragrant.
Add Pork:
Add the ground pork to the wok. Break it up with a spatula and cook until it's no longer pink.
Season:
Add oyster sauce, soy sauce, fish sauce, and sugar to the pork. Stir well to combine and let it cook for another minute or two.
Add Basil Leaves:
Once the pork is cooked through and the flavors are well combined, add the basil leaves to the wok. Stir-fry for a minute or until the basil leaves are wilted and infused with the flavors of the dish.
Adjust Seasoning:
Taste the dish and adjust the seasoning if necessary. You can add more soy sauce, fish sauce, or sugar according to your taste preferences.
Serve:
Serve the Pad Kra Pao Moo over jasmine rice. You can also top it with a fried egg if desired.


Ingredients


1 lb ground meat, 1 cup finely chopped fresh herbs, 1/2 cup chopped green onions 1/4 cup finely chopped red onion 2-3 tablespoons roasted rice powder, 2-3 tablespoons fish sauce, 2-3 tablespoons lime juice, 1-2 teaspoons chili powder or finely chopped fresh chilies, 1 teaspoon sugar, Lettuce leaves or cabbage leaves for serving
For the roasted rice powder:
Heat a dry pan over medium heat. Add uncooked sticky rice to the pan and stir constantly until the rice turns golden brown. Allow the rice to cool and then grind it into a coarse powder using a mortar and pestle or a spice grinder.

Cooking instructions


Cook the meat:
In a skillet over medium heat, cook the ground meat until it's fully cooked and browned. If there's excess fat, you can drain it.
Prepare the dressing:
In a small bowl, mix together fish sauce, lime juice, chili powder, and sugar. Adjust the quantities to suit your taste.
Combine the ingredients:
In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked meat with chopped herbs, green onions, and red onion.
Add the dressing:
Pour the dressing over the meat and herb mixture. Toss everything together until well combined.
Add roasted rice powder:
Sprinkle the roasted rice powder over the salad and mix it in. This adds a nutty flavor and a bit of texture.
Serve:
Arrange lettuce or cabbage leaves on a serving platter. Spoon the laab mixture onto the leaves.
Garnish and serve:
Garnish with extra herbs and a wedge of lime. Serve immediately and enjoy your delicious Laab


Ingredients


For the curry paste:
4 dried red chili peppers, soaked in hot water 1 shallot, chopped 4 garlic cloves 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced 1 tablespoon coriander seeds, toasted 1 tablespoon turmeric powder 1 teaspoon cumin 1 teaspoon paprika
For the soup:
2 tablespoons vegetable oil 2 tablespoons red curry paste (from above) 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk 3 cups chicken or vegetable broth 1.5 pounds chicken thighs or beef, thinly sliced 2 tablespoons soy sauce 1 tablespoon fish sauce 1 tablespoon palm sugar or brown sugar Salt, to taste
For serving:
Fresh egg noodles or rice noodles, cooked according to package instructions Lime wedges Red onion, thinly sliced Fresh cilantro, chopped Crispy fried noodles or shallots

Cooking instructions


Prepare the curry paste:
In a blender or food processor, combine all the curry paste ingredients and blend until you get a smooth paste.
Cook the curry paste:
Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add 2 tablespoons of the red curry paste and saut√© for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
Add coconut milk and broth:
Pour in the coconut milk and chicken or vegetable broth. Stir well to combine.
Cook the protein:
Add the sliced chicken or beef to the pot. Simmer until the meat is cooked through, stirring occasionally.
Season the soup:
Add soy sauce, fish sauce, palm sugar, and salt to taste. Adjust the seasoning as needed.
Prepare noodles:
Cook the fresh or rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
Serve:
Divide the noodles among serving bowls. Ladle the soup over the noodles.
Garnish:
Garnish the soup with lime wedges, sliced red onion, chopped cilantro, and crispy fried noodles or shallots if desired.
