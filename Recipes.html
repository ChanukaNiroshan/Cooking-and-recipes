Ingredients

1 cup Basmati rice, 2 cups water, Salt to taste

2 tablespoons vegetable oil 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 teaspoon ginger, grated 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste) 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, potatoes, etc.) 1 cup coconut milk or water Salt to taste Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)

Cooking instructions

Rinse the Basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This helps remove excess starch.

Optional: Soak the rice in water for about 15-30 minutes. This helps the rice cook more evenly

In a pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil, Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling water, Add salt to taste, Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed, Fluff the rice with a fork.

In a pan, heat vegetable oil over medium heat, Add chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent, Add minced garlic and grated ginger, sauté for another minute

Add cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and red chili powder. Cook the spices for a minute to release their flavors.

Add the mixed vegetables and stir well to coat them with the spices, Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables begin to soften

Pour in coconut milk or water. Bring the mixture to a simmer, Season with salt to taste, Let it simmer until the vegetables are fully cooked and the curry reaches the desired consistency

Serve the vegetable curry over the steamed Basmati rice, Garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired.






Ingredients

2 cups raw rice 1/2 cup cooked rice 1/4 cup urad dal (black gram lentils) 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds 1 teaspoon active dry yeast (optional, for fermentation) Salt to taste Coconut oil or any vegetable oil Extra oil or ghee for greasing the hopper pan

Cooking instructions

Rinse the raw rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds. Soak them in water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight, Grind the soaked rice, urad dal, fenugreek seeds, and cooked rice into a smooth batter. Add water as needed to achieve a pouring consistency, If you prefer, you can ferment the batter by adding active dry yeast and letting it sit in a warm place for 6-8 hours or overnight. Fermentation is optional but enhances the flavor

Heat a hopper pan or appam pan over medium heat, Grease the pan with a little oil or ghee.

Pour a ladleful of batter into the center of the pan, Lift the pan and swirl it around to spread the batter thinly, coating the sides to create a bowl shape.

Cover the pan with a lid and let the hopper cook over low to medium heat, Cook until the edges become crispy, and the center is soft. This usually takes about 2-3 minutes

Carefully remove the hopper from the pan using a spatula, Repeat the process with the remaining batter.




Ingredients

2 cups rice flour, 1 1/2 to 2 cups hot water, 1/2 teaspoon salt

Cooking instructions

In a large mixing bowl, add rice flour and salt (if using), Gradually add hot water while stirring continuously to form a smooth, lump-free dough. The consistency should be such that the dough is firm enough to hold its shape but soft enough to be extruded through the string hopper press.

Once the dough is ready, fill the string hopper press or idiyappam maker with the dough. If you don't have a string hopper press, you can use a potato ricer with a fine-holed disc.

Hold the string hopper press over the idli steamer or a large pot of boiling water. Press the dough through the small holes in a circular motion to create thin, delicate noodles. You can make a single layer or create a nest-like structure.

If using an idli steamer, steam the string hoppers for about 5-7 minutes or until they are cooked through and firm. If you don't have an idli steamer, you can improvise by placing a steaming rack over a pot of boiling water. Place the string hoppers on the rack, cover with a lid, and steam.

Once cooked, carefully remove the string hoppers from the steamer using a spatula.
